\subsection{Parameter-Independent Projective Ordering}
  \label{subsec:parameter-independent-relaxation}

  Physical evolution appears only at the effective level, as an ordered
  sequence of projected $\chi$ configurations
  $(\chi_{\mathrm{eff},\lambda})$, where $\lambda$ is a strictly monotonic
  ordering parameter labeling admissible stages of the projective cascade.
  The ordering parameter~$\lambda$ does not represent time; it serves as an
  index ordering projected configurations once a macroscopic spacetime
  description becomes applicable.

  Admissible projected descriptions are constrained by an effective
  ordering condition:
  \begin{equation}
    \mathcal{D}_{\lambda}\chi_{\mathrm{eff}}
    = \mathcal{R}[\chi_{\mathrm{eff}}] ,
  \end{equation}
  where $\mathcal{R}[\chi_{\mathrm{eff}}]$ denotes an effective projective
  ordering functional characterizing the admissible progression of projected
  configurations.
  This relation is a consistency condition on admissible projections, not a
  fundamental dynamical law.
  The functional~$\mathcal{R}$ is defined only in regimes admitting a stable
  geometric interpretation.

  Quantities commonly interpreted as temporal derivatives arise exclusively
  within effective descriptions.
  Within this framework, the projective ordering does not occur \emph{in}
  time; the ordering of projected configurations defines what is
  operationally identified as physical duration.

\subsection{The Casimir Effect}
  \label{subsec:casimir-effect}

  When material boundaries impose structural constraints on local projectability,
  certain effective descriptions become incompatible with the boundary conditions,
  reducing the set of admissible projective configurations between the boundaries.
  The Casimir effect arises from this asymmetry: a difference in the density of admissible
  effective reprojections between the interior and exterior of the confining geometry,
  manifesting as a net pressure on the surfaces.
  No fundamental vacuum energy density or propagating vacuum modes are required.

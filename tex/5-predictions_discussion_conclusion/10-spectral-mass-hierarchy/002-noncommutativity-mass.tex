\subsection{Non-Commutativity as a Source of Mass}
  \label{sec:noncommutativity-mass}

  Torsion acts as a dynamical constraint that competes with relaxation
  transport through the projection fiber.
  Mass amplification arises when torsional and relaxation generators
  fail to share a common spectral basis.

  \subsubsection*{Inhibition of Relaxation}
    \label{sec:inhibition-relaxation}

    Let $\Omega_w$ denote the effective torsion operator associated with
    winding number~$w$.
    For the fundamental lepton configuration ($w=1$),
    relaxation and torsion remain spectrally compatible:
    \begin{equation}
      [\Delta^{(0)}_G,\Omega_{1}] = 0 .
    \end{equation}
    In this case, relaxation proceeds uniformly along the projection fiber.

    For higher-winding configurations ($w\ge 2$),
    torsional constraints become spectrally frustrated:
    \begin{equation}
      [\Delta^{(0)}_G,\Omega_{w}] \neq 0
      \qquad (w\ge 2).
    \end{equation}
    The absence of a common eigenbasis prevents uniform relaxation and
    induces anisotropic spectral compression within the admissible subspace.
    This obstruction manifests macroscopically as amplified inertial mass.

    The associated torsional action is defined as a purely spectral invariant:
    \begin{equation}
      \mathcal{A}(w) \;\equiv\;
      \frac12 \ln\!\left(
                     \frac{\det(\Delta^{(0)}_G+\Omega_{w})}
                     {\det(\Delta^{(0)}_G)}
      \right),
      \label{eq:Aw-fredholm}
    \end{equation}
    where the determinant is understood in the zeta-regularized sense.
    This invariant quantifies global spectral distortion but does not by
    itself encode dynamical stability selection.

  \subsubsection*{The Pisano Ratio as a Dynamical Stability Criterion}
    \label{sec:pisano-ratio}

    For $w=2$, the projection fiber ceases to be spectrally isotropic.
    The first spectral multiplet of $S^3$ admits a natural Hopf-compatible
    decomposition into a fiber-aligned singlet and a transverse triplet,
    inducing a splitting
    \begin{equation}
      \Pi = \Pi_{\parallel} \oplus \Pi_{\perp}.
    \end{equation}

    This splitting corresponds to two competing dynamical modes of the
    relaxation flow.
    The relevant quantities are not the static eigenvalues of
    $\Delta^{(0)}_G+\Omega_{2}$,
    but the effective rotational frequencies generated by the torsional
    operator within the reduced multiplet.

    Denote by $\lambda_{\parallel}$ and $\lambda_{\perp}$ the two effective
    frequencies associated with the torsion-induced evolution in the
    subspaces $\Pi_{\parallel}$ and $\Pi_{\perp}$.
    Dynamical stability selects the most irrational admissible ratio
    between these frequencies, in analogy with Kolmogorov–Arnold–Moser (KAM) criteria:
    \begin{equation}
      \frac{\lambda_{\parallel}}{\lambda_{\perp}}
      = \varphi,
      \label{eq:beta-golden}
    \end{equation}
    where $\varphi=(1+\sqrt5)/2$ is the golden ratio.

    Equation~\eqref{eq:beta-golden} is therefore a dynamical selection
    principle imposed on the torsional generator $\Omega_{2}$ within the
    first multiplet.
    It should not be interpreted as a generic spectral identity of
    $\Delta^{(0)}_G+\Omega_{2}$ in the weak-torsion regime.

  \subsubsection*{Leptonic Spectrum Synthesis}
    \label{sec:leptonic-synthesis}

    For the muon, non-commutative torsion in the $w=2$ sector combines:

    \begin{itemize}
      \item[(i)] the topologically fixed spectral ratio
      \(
      \lambda_2/\lambda_1 = 8/3
      \),
      \item[(ii)] the dynamical Pisano selection
      \(
      \lambda_{\parallel}/\lambda_{\perp}=\varphi
      \),
      \item[(iii)] the universal coupling factor $3/(2\alpha)$.
    \end{itemize}

    This yields the parameter-free prediction
    \begin{equation}
      \boxed{
        \frac{m_\mu}{m_e}
        = \sqrt{\frac{\lambda_{2}}{\lambda_{1}}}
        \cdot \frac{3}{2\alpha}
        \cdot \frac{1}{\varphi}
      }
      \qquad\text{with}\qquad
      \frac{\lambda_2}{\lambda_1} = \frac{8}{3}.
      \label{eq:muon-ratio-final}
    \end{equation}

    The robustness of the ratio $\lambda_2/\lambda_1 = 8/3$ is demonstrated
    independently in Appendix~\ref{sec:spectral_ratio_derivation},
    while the Pisano factor arises from a dynamical stability condition
    applied to the torsional generator within the first spectral multiplet.

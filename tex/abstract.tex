\abstract{
  Cosmochrony is a pre-geometric relational framework in which time,
  spacetime geometry, and physical observables emerge from the
  irreversible ordering of an effective reprojection sequence
  constrained by a single underlying substrate, denoted $\chi$.
  The framework adopts a principle of ontological minimalism,
  postulating no fundamental spacetime, metric, or quantum structure.
  Instead, effective geometric and dynamical descriptions arise only
  through coarse-grained, generally non-injective projections of
  admissible configurations constrained by $\chi$.

  The admissible sector of the relational substrate is characterized
  by a spectral structure defined by a relational operator acting on
  the configuration space of $\chi$.
  Physical descriptions correspond to projectable modes selected by
  a spectral admissibility condition.
  Within this framework, temporal ordering is identified with intrinsic
  monotonic ordering of the effective reprojection sequence, while spatial relations
  and curvature emerge as effective summaries of relational correlations.

  Bounded-response regimes arise from saturation of relational
  admissibility constraints, leading to Born--Infeld-like effective dynamics
  without introducing additional fundamental fields.
  In this context, the speed of light and Planck's constant appear as
  complementary bounds on projectability and resolvability rather
  than as primitive constants.

  Matter, inertia, and electric charge correspond to stable or
  metastable invariants of the effective dynamics associated with
  localized spectral configurations of the projected description.
  The topological structure of the projection fiber further constrains
  admissible configurations: electric charge is identified with a
  winding invariant of the $U(1)$ fiber, isolated magnetic monopoles
  are excluded in the canonical admissible sector, and parity
  asymmetry arises from a projective chirality induced by the
  non-injective nature of the projection.

  Quantum indeterminacy, entanglement, and violations of Bell
  inequalities arise as structural consequences of non-injective
  projection, without requiring superluminal influences or
  fundamental wavefunction collapse.

  At macroscopic scales, gravitation emerges as a collective effect of
  inhibited effective evolution, recovering general-relativistic phenomenology
  in appropriate regimes.
  At galactic and cosmological scales, the same bounded-response
  mechanism provides an effective interpretation of flat galaxy
  rotation curves and environment-dependent Hubble parameter
  inference without invoking dark matter particles or a fundamental
  dark energy component.

  Cosmochrony is presented as a unifying structural framework rather
  than a replacement for established physical theories.
  Its aim is to clarify how time, geometry, quantum phenomena,
  and cosmological behavior may consistently emerge from a common
  pre-geometric relational substrate and to organize a set of
  testable effective predictions developed in companion works.
}

\part{Gravitation and Cosmology}
  \label{part:gravitation-and-cosmology}

  This part addresses gravitation and cosmology as collective, large-scale
  manifestations of constrained projective ordering rather than as
  fundamental interactions.
  Gravitation is shown to arise from the local inhibition of the admissible
  projective ordering rate induced by particle excitations, leading to an
  emergent curvature description consistent with general relativity in
  appropriate regimes.

  Cosmological expansion is interpreted as a global consequence of the
  monotone accumulation of projected spectral structure, without invoking
  inflation, dark matter particles, or a fundamental dark energy component.
  Observable phenomena such as galactic rotation curves, gravitational
  lensing, cosmic voids, and the Hubble tension are reinterpreted as
  effective responses to structural saturation and projective compensation.

  This part connects the effective projective structure of~$\chi$ to
  astrophysical and cosmological observables, providing the bridge between
  foundational principles and large-scale data.

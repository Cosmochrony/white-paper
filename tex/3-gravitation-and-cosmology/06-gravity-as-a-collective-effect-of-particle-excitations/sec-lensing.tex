\subsection{Effective Gravitational Lensing}
\label{subsec:effective-lensing}

In the weak-field and thin-lens regimes, the lens equation is
\begin{equation}
  \boldsymbol{\beta}
  =\boldsymbol{\theta}
  -\frac{D_{ls}}{D_s}\,
  \nabla_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}\psi(\boldsymbol{\theta}),
\end{equation}
with the lensing potential
\begin{equation}
  \psi(\boldsymbol{\theta})
  =\frac{2D_{ls}}{c^2 D_l D_s}
  \int \Phi_{\mathrm{eff}}\!
    \left(D_l\boldsymbol{\theta},z\right)\,dz .
\end{equation}
The effective potential~$\Phi_{\mathrm{eff}}$ is a geometric descriptor
encoding collective constraints of the projected $\chi$-substrate.
All observable lensing quantities (convergence, shear, magnification,
caustics) follow in the usual way.
No additional dark matter component is required: lensing directly probes
the emergent geometry.

\subsection{Analytical Solutions of the
\texorpdfstring{$\chi$}{Ï‡}-Field Dynamics}
\label{subsec:analytical-solutions}

Explicit solutions of
\begin{equation}
  \partial_t \chi
  = c \sqrt{1 - \frac{|\nabla \chi|^2}{c^2}}
  \label{eq:chi_effective_evolution}
\end{equation}
illustrate the causal structure and relaxational character.

\paragraph{Homogeneous relaxation.}
For $\nabla\chi = 0$: $\chi(t) = \chi_0 + c\,t$, representing
uniform global relaxation.

\paragraph{Gradient-saturated profiles.}
Configurations with $|\partial_r\chi| = c$ satisfy
$\partial_t\chi = 0$ (local freezing), taking the form
$\chi(r) = \chi_0 \pm c\,r$.
These idealize horizons and maximally constrained regions.

\paragraph{Linear relaxation fronts.}
$\chi(x,t) = \chi_0 + c\,t \pm v\,x$ with $|v| < c$ satisfies
\eqref{eq:chi_effective_evolution} identically, describing
kinematic boundaries of the relaxation.

\paragraph{Absence of linear wave solutions.}
Small perturbations do not propagate as oscillatory modes---the dynamics
is purely relaxational.
Apparent wave-like phenomena arise only at the effective level through
collective excitations.

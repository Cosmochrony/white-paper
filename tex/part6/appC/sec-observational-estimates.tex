\subsection{Relation to Observational Units and Numerical Estimates}
\label{subsec:observational-estimates}

\subsubsection*{Normalization of the
\texorpdfstring{$\chi$}{Ï‡} Field}
\label{subsec:normalization-of-the-chi-field}

$\chi(t_0)$ is identified with the characteristic geometric scale
governing large-scale expansion, via $a(t) \propto \chi(t)$.

\subsubsection*{Emergent Gravitational Coupling}
\label{subsec:K0-chic-constraints}

$G$ emerges from the constitutive relation:
$K_0 \chi_c^2 \sim c^4/(16\pi G)$.

\subsubsection*{Hubble Constant}
\label{subsec:hubble-constant}

In the homogeneous limit,
$H_0 \simeq c/\chi(t_0)$ yields
$\chi(t_0) \sim 4 \times 10^{26}$~m, of order the Hubble radius.

\subsubsection*{Age of the Universe}
\label{subsec:age-of-the-universe}

With $\dot{\chi} \simeq c$,
$t_0 \simeq \chi(t_0)/c
  \sim 4 \times 10^{17}$~s ($\approx 13.8$~Gyr).

\subsubsection*{Redshift Interpretation}
\label{subsec:redshift-interpretation}

$1 + z
  = \chi(t_{\mathrm{obs}})/\chi(t_{\mathrm{emit}})$.

\subsubsection*{CMB Scale}
\label{subsec:cosmic-microwave-background-scale}

At recombination ($z_{\mathrm{rec}} \simeq 1100$),
$\chi(t_{\mathrm{rec}})
  \simeq \chi(t_0)/(1 + z_{\mathrm{rec}})$.

\subsubsection*{Orders of Magnitude and Robustness}
\label{subsec:orders-of-magnitude-and-robustness}

All estimates rely solely on observed quantities and bounded relaxation
dynamics.
No fine-tuning or additional degrees of freedom are assumed.

\subsubsection*{Summary}
\label{subsec:summary}

Cosmochrony reproduces key cosmological scales without introducing new
fundamental parameters.

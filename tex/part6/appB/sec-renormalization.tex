\subsection{Renormalization of Substrate Parameters}
\label{subsec:renormalization}

Bare parameters ($K_0$, $\chi_c$) are universal substrate invariants;
effective parameters ($K_{\mathrm{eff}}$, $\chi_{\mathrm{eff}}$)
emerge through coarse-graining
(Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-simulation}).
The universality of $\hbar$ and the spectral invariant
$\alpha_{\mathrm{spec}}$ stems from their dependence on bare ratios,
invariant under projective scaling within a given relaxation epoch.
Dimensionless couplings such as $\alpha_{\mathrm{EM}}$ inherit no
arbitrariness from substrate parameters: the electric charge is a
geometric invariant of the soliton's spectral embedding relative
to~$\Pi$, so the dependence on $K_0$ cancels in dimensionless ratios.

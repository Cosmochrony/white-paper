\subsection{Estimates of
\texorpdfstring{$\chi$}{Ï‡}-Field Parameters}
\label{subsec:chi-parameters}

Effective parameters characterize the projected relaxation operator and
do not represent fundamental degrees of freedom.
We distinguish bare parameters ($K_{0,\mathrm{bare}}$,
$\chi_{c,\mathrm{bare}}$), which are universal substrate invariants,
from effective parameters ($K_{0,\mathrm{eff}}$,
$\chi_{c,\mathrm{eff}}$) encoding the local density of relaxation
constraints
(Section~\ref{subsec:renormalization-hbar}).

The emergent gravitational constant is driven by the ratio
$K_{0,\mathrm{eff}}/\chi_{c,\mathrm{eff}}^2$.
Two illustrative normalizations highlight the scale-dependency:
Planck-scale identification
($\chi_c \sim \ell_P$) yields
$K_0 \sim 10^{93}\,\mathrm{m}^{-2}$;
cosmological-scale identification
($\chi_c \sim c/H_0$) yields
$K_0 \sim 10^{-52}\,\mathrm{m}^{-2}$.
Both are internally consistent, suggesting the dynamics are spectrally
self-similar: $G$ remains constant across observational scales provided
the ratio of effective stiffness to correlation length is preserved.

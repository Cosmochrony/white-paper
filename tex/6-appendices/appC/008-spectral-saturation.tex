\subsection{Spectral Interpretation of the Galactic Saturation Regime}
\label{app:spectral-saturation-galaxies}

In projectable regimes, the collective response is characterized by an
effective elliptic operator $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{eff}}$ with spectrum
$\lambda_n$.
Beyond a critical scale, the smallest eigenvalue approaches a saturation
threshold~$\lambda_c$, corresponding to the stiffness
threshold~$K_c$ of
Appendix~\ref{app:substrate-origin-phi-eff}.
The effective acceleration scale satisfies
$a_0 \sim \lambda_c/\tau_\chi^2$.
When the spectrum becomes marginally scale-invariant near~$\lambda_c$,
the Green function behaves as $G(r) \propto \ln r$, providing a spectral
explanation for flat rotation curves.
At scales where $\lambda_{\min} \ll \lambda_c$, the dense spectrum
recovers the familiar $1/r$ Newtonian behavior.

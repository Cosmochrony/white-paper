\subsection{Renormalization and the Universality of
\texorpdfstring{$\hbar$}{‚Ñè}}
\label{subsec:renormalization-hbar}

We distinguish bare substrate parameters from their effective
counterparts:
\begin{equation}
  \hbar_\chi
  \equiv
  \frac{c^3}
       {K_{0,\mathrm{bare}}\,\chi_{c,\mathrm{bare}}}.
\end{equation}
Effective parameters ($K_{0,\mathrm{eff}}$,
$\chi_{c,\mathrm{eff}}$) incorporate the local density of relaxation
constraints.
No observable variation of $\hbar$ arises within a fixed relaxation
epoch, since bare parameters remain invariant.
The perceived universality of $\hbar$ stems from its exclusive
dependence on the ratio of bare quantities, invariant under projective
scaling.
